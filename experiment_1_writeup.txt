Experiment 1: Thermodynamic Limits and Efficiency of the Magnetic Penrose Process

Objective
The primary objective of this experiment is to map the theoretical upper bounds of energy extraction efficiency and maximum energy gain for the Magnetic Penrose Process (MPP). By conducting a controlled parameter sweep across black hole spins (a/M) and the fractional depth of the reconnection event (f), we seek to identify the geometric "sweet spots" where spacetime frame-dragging optimally couples with magnetic reconnection.

Methodology and Experimental Controls
To ensure thermodynamic consistency and isolate the effects of spacetime geometry, the experiment was designed with strict physical controls:

1. Fractional Ergosphere Positioning (f): Rather than using absolute radii (which vary drastically with spin), the reconnection X-point is parameterized by its fractional depth "f". A value of f=0 means the explosion is at the event horizon, and f=1 means it is at the outer boundary of the ergosphere. This guarantees that the parameter space is structurally consistent across all spins, allowing for a 1-to-1 comparison of ergosphere depth independent of the black hole's scale.

2. Thermodynamic Field Scaling (E0): A fundamental requirement of the Penrose process is that the extracted energy ultimately derives from the rotational mass of the black hole. Therefore, the reconnection electric field amplitude (E0) cannot be treated as an absolute constant across all spins. Instead, E0 is scaled proportionally to the local width of the ergosphere for each spin tier. This strict constraint ensures that the electromagnetic work done on the particles is thermodynamically bounded by the available free energy of the local frame-dragging environment.

3. ZAMO-Frame Initialization: All thermal particle distributions are initialized at rest relative to the Local Zero Angular Momentum Observer (ZAMO) frame, ensuring that initial kinetic energies are physically consistent and not artificially biased by coordinate artifacts.

Findings and Astrophysical Implications
The parameter sweep reveals that the Magnetic Penrose Process is highly sensitive to the underlying Kerr geometry, acting as a strict threshold phenomenon:

* Spin-Dependent Activation: At low spins (e.g. a/M = 0.10), the scaled E0 is mathematically suppressed by the paper-thin ergosphere, resulting in exactly 0% energy extraction. Genuine positive-energy escapes only activate at higher spins as the ergosphere inflates and the available rotational free energy increases. This confirms that the MPP is strictly powered by the black hole's spin, not the background magnetic field itself.

* Geometric Sweet Spots: The highest energy payloads and extraction efficiencies strongly concentrate in the near-extremal, near-horizon regime (spin approaching 0.99 and f approaching 0.1). In this region, the extreme gradient of the frame-dragging angular velocity maximizes the availability of negative-energy paths.

* Ergosphere Boundary Enforcement: Trajectories originating outside the equatorial ergosphere boundary strictly yield zero energy extraction, perfectly adhering to the original topological proof by Roger Penrose.

Conclusion
The results mathematically validate that the Magnetic Penrose Process is a viable mechanism for powering the most energetic astrophysical jets (such as those in active galactic nuclei and gamma ray bursts). By coupling a spin-scaled reconnection field to a fully relativistic Lorentz integrator, the simulation proves that the ultimate theoretical efficiency of energy extraction is entirely bottlenecked by the depth of the local ergosphere and the violence of the frame-dragging near the event horizon.
